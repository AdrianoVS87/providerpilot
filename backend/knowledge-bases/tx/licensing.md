# Texas Childcare Licensing Requirements

## Regulatory Authority
- Texas Health and Human Services Commission (HHSC)
- Child Care Licensing (CCL) division
- Governed by Texas Administrative Code Title 26, Part 1

## Home-Based Daycare Types

### Listed Family Home (1-3 unrelated children)
- No license required if caring for 1-3 unrelated children
- Must register with HHSC
- Background check required
- Basic health and safety standards apply

### Registered Child-Care Home (4-6 children, or 1-12 with assistant)
- **Chapter 747**: Minimum Standards for Child-Care Homes
- Must register with HHSC
- Background check and fingerprinting required
- 24-hour pre-service training
- First aid and CPR certification
- Annual 30-hour continuing education
- Fire safety inspection
- Home inspection by licensing representative

### Licensed Child-Care Center (7+ children)
- **Chapter 746**: Minimum Standards for Child-Care Centers
- Full license from HHSC required
- Director qualifications: 2+ years experience, CDA or equivalent
- Staff-to-child ratios:
  - Infants (0-17 months): 1:4
  - Toddlers (18-35 months): 1:9 (with ages mixed up to 35 months: 1:9)
  - Preschool (3-4 years): 1:15
  - School-age (5-13 years): 1:22
- Square footage: 30 sq ft per child (indoor), 80 sq ft per child (outdoor)
- Annual fire and health inspections
- Liability insurance recommended

## Application Process
1. Attend orientation session (online or in-person)
2. Complete application (Form 2911 for homes, Form 2910 for centers)
3. Submit background check (FBI + state) for all adults in home
4. Complete pre-service training (24 hours minimum)
5. Pass home/facility inspection
6. Obtain fire marshal clearance
7. Submit medical statement for provider
8. Receive license/registration (typically 4-6 weeks)

## Required Documents
- Government-issued photo ID
- Social Security card
- Proof of CPR/First Aid certification (pediatric)
- TB test results (within last 12 months)
- Background check clearance (CBC)
- Proof of liability insurance (centers)
- Floor plan of facility
- Emergency preparedness plan
- Parent handbook

## Key Compliance Areas
- Safe sleep practices (Back to Sleep)
- Medication administration policy
- Nutrition and meal planning (USDA CACFP guidelines)
- Outdoor play requirements (minimum 2 hours/day for full-day programs)
- Discipline policy (no corporal punishment)
- Transportation safety (if applicable)
- Emergency drills (fire monthly, severe weather quarterly)

## Costs
- Registration fee: $35 (homes)
- License fee: $35 (centers, annual)
- Background check: ~$50 per person
- Training costs: varies ($100-500 for pre-service)

## Renewal
- Annual renewal required
- Continuing education: 30 hours/year for providers, 24 hours/year for staff
- Background check renewal every 2 years
- Inspection at least annually (unannounced visits may occur)
