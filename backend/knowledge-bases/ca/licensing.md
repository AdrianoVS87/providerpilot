# California Childcare Licensing Requirements

## Regulatory Authority
- California Department of Social Services (CDSS)
- Community Care Licensing Division (CCLD)
- Governed by California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 12

## Home-Based Daycare Types

### Small Family Child Care Home (1-8 children)
- License from CDSS required
- Provider must be 18+ years old
- Up to 8 children (including provider's own under 10)
- No more than 6 children under age 6 at any time
- Provider may work alone

### Large Family Child Care Home (7-14 children)
- License from CDSS required
- Up to 14 children (including provider's own under 10)
- Must have at least one assistant
- Additional space requirements
- Provider must have experience or education in child development

## Staff-to-Child Ratios (Centers - Title 5)
- Infants (0-18 months): 1:3
- Toddlers (18-30 months): 1:4
- Preschool (30 months - 5 years): 1:8
- School-age (5-12 years): 1:14

## Square Footage Requirements
- Indoor: 35 sq ft per child
- Outdoor: 75 sq ft per child
- Napping space must be separate or partitioned

## Application Process
1. Attend Community Care Licensing orientation
2. Complete application (LIC 200 series forms)
3. Submit Livescan fingerprinting for all adults in household
4. Complete background check (DOJ + FBI)
5. Complete health screening (LIC 503)
6. Obtain fire clearance from local fire authority
7. Pass facility inspection
8. Complete 16 hours of health and safety training (preventive health, nutrition, CPR/First Aid)
9. Obtain Trustline registration
10. Receive license (typically 6-12 weeks)

## Required Documents
- Government-issued photo ID
- Social Security number
- Livescan fingerprint results
- Criminal Record Statement (LIC 508)
- Health Screening Report (LIC 503) - physical exam + TB test
- CPR/First Aid certification (pediatric)
- Proof of immunizations for provider's own children
- Fire clearance
- Facility sketch/floor plan
- Emergency disaster plan
- Earthquake preparedness plan (California-specific)

## Key Compliance Areas (Title 22)
- Immunization records for all children
- Personal Rights (LIC 613A)
- Admission agreements
- Safe sleep practices (SIDS prevention)
- Medication administration (authorized by parent, logged)
- Outdoor supervision requirements
- Earthquake preparedness (California-specific)
- Posting requirements (license, emergency numbers, menus)
- Mandated reporter obligations

## Training Requirements
- 16 hours health and safety training (initial)
- Pediatric CPR and First Aid (before license issuance)
- 21 hours/year continuing education
- Topics must include: child development, nutrition, safety

## Costs
- Application fee: $25
- License fee: $1 per child capacity
- Livescan fingerprinting: ~$75-100 per person
- Fire clearance: varies by jurisdiction ($50-200)
- Training: varies ($150-500)

## Renewal
- License renewal every 3 years (family child care)
- Annual inspection (unannounced)
- Background check every 5 years or upon change
- Continuing education: 21 hours/year

## Unique California Requirements
- Earthquake preparedness plan mandatory
- Prop 65 chemical exposure posting
- AB 2370 lead testing requirements
- Pesticide notification requirements
- Bilingual posting of parent rights (English + Spanish)
