# New York Childcare Licensing Requirements

## Regulatory Authority
- New York State Office of Children and Family Services (OCFS)
- Division of Child Care Services (DCCS)
- Governed by Title 18 NYCRR (Parts 413-418)

## Home-Based Daycare Types

### Family Day Care Home (3-6 children)
- **Registration** with OCFS (not a license)
- Up to 6 children (including provider's own under 6)
- No more than 2 children under age 2
- Provider may work alone
- Part 416 of Title 18 NYCRR

### Group Family Day Care Home (7-12 children)
- **License** from OCFS required
- Up to 12 children (including provider's own under 6)
- Must have at least one assistant when 7+ children present
- No more than 4 children under age 2 (with assistant)
- Part 416 of Title 18 NYCRR

### Day Care Center (7+ children, non-residential)
- **License** from OCFS required
- Part 418-1 of Title 18 NYCRR
- Director must have specific education/experience qualifications
- Annual license renewal

## Staff-to-Child Ratios (Centers)
- Infants (0-6 months): 1:3
- Infants (6-12 months): 1:4
- Toddlers (12-24 months): 1:5
- Toddlers (24-36 months): 1:6
- Preschool (3-4 years): 1:7
- Pre-K (4-5 years): 1:8
- School-age (5-10 years): 1:9
- School-age (10+ years): 1:10

## Square Footage Requirements
- Indoor: 35 sq ft per child (centers)
- Indoor: 35 sq ft per child (family/group homes, for rooms used by children)
- Outdoor: not specified per child but must be adequate and safe

## Application Process
1. Attend orientation (required for all new providers)
2. Complete 15-hour Health and Safety Training (approved by OCFS)
3. Submit application (OCFS-LDSS-7001 for homes)
4. Complete background check (SCR, SEL, criminal history)
5. All household members 18+ must be fingerprinted
6. Medical statement from physician (within 12 months)
7. CPR/First Aid certification (pediatric)
8. Fire safety inspection
9. Home/facility inspection by OCFS licensor
10. Receive registration/license (typically 8-12 weeks)

## Required Documents
- Government-issued photo ID
- Social Security number
- Background check (Statewide Central Register - SCR database check)
- Staff Exclusion List (SEL) clearance
- FBI fingerprint check (all adults in household)
- Medical statement (physical exam within 12 months)
- TB test or risk assessment
- CPR/First Aid certification (pediatric, within 2 years)
- Proof of 15-hour Health and Safety training
- Floor plan of facility
- Emergency evacuation plan
- Written safety plan

## Key Compliance Areas
- SCR (State Central Register) clearance for all caregivers
- Safe sleep practices (SIDS prevention, ABCs of Safe Sleep)
- Supervision requirements (children never left unsupervised)
- Medication administration (written parental consent required)
- Immunization records for all children
- Daily health check of children upon arrival
- Mandated reporter obligations (Child Abuse/Maltreatment)
- OCFS posting requirements (license, emergency numbers, parents' rights)
- No firearms in facility during operating hours (homes)

## Training Requirements
- 15 hours Health and Safety training (pre-service, within first 6 months)
  - Topics: CPR, First Aid, infection control, SIDS, nutrition, child abuse, medication, safety
- 30 hours/2 years continuing education (all providers)
- CPR/First Aid renewal every 2 years
- Annual training topics mandated by OCFS

## Costs
- Application fee: None (registration is free for family homes)
- License fee: $50 (group family homes), varies for centers
- Background check: ~$25-75 per person
- Training: $100-300 for 15-hour course
- First Aid/CPR: ~$50-100

## Renewal
- Registration renewal every 2 years (family day care)
- License renewal annually (group family, centers)
- Inspection prior to renewal (announced or unannounced)
- Background check renewal every 5 years
- Continuing education compliance checked at renewal

## Unique New York Requirements
- SCR (State Central Register) database check — unique to NY
- Staff Exclusion List (SEL) check — unique to NY
- Corporal punishment explicitly prohibited by regulation
- Kosher/Halal meal accommodation requirements in NYC
- NYC has additional Department of Health requirements (Article 47)
- NYC family day care homes regulated by NYC DOHMH, not OCFS
